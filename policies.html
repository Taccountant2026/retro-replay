Information

Everything you need to know about ordering, returns, warranty, and device condition.

Refurbished Console Standards

Every PlayStation 2 console sold by Retro Replay undergoes a full professional refurbishment process. This includes internal cleaning, fan cleaning or replacement, thermal refresh, laser testing, port inspection, exterior restoration, and full functional validation.

Our goal is to deliver the most reliable PS2 experience possible — whether completely original for collectors, or enhanced with HDD, homebrew features, and performance improvements for enthusiasts.

Condition Guide

These devices are now between 20–25 years old, and as such, normal cosmetic ageing should be expected. All units are graded and cleaned, but minor scratches, small case marks, or light use wear may still be present. Everything we ship is fully tested and in proper working order at the time of dispatch.

Warranty & Returns

Because these consoles are vintage electronics, Retro Replay provides a fair and protective warranty policy that covers genuine faults while keeping expectations realistic for hardware of this age.

✔ 14-Day Functional Guarantee

All consoles include a 14-day functional warranty covering issues present on arrival, such as:

  • Failure to power on
  • Major disc-read failure (disc models)
  • HDD not detected (HDD models)
  • Serious operational faults present from delivery

✔ What Is Not Covered

Due to the age of the hardware, the following are outside warranty:

  • Long-term age-related wear (laser age, PSU aging, fan wear)
  • Cosmetic wear after customer use
  • Damage caused by opening or modifying the console
  • Issues caused by unsupported drives, software, or game backups
  • Damage from drops, misuse, or incorrect power use

✔ Returns Policy

  • Returns accepted within 14 days of delivery.
  • Buyer covers return shipping unless the console arrives faulty.
  • Refunds are issued once the device is received and inspected.

Retro Replay fully tests every console prior to shipping, but because these systems are 20+ years old, no long-term guarantee can be offered. Performance may vary slightly between units, which is normal for vintage PlayStation 2 hardware.
